About

I build AI products, not just manage them.

I'm a product manager who builds AI products. Right now I lead data and AI platforms at TD Bank, where I own strategy and delivery for tools used by over 100,000 people. Before that I spent three years on ML-powered personalization serving 2.5 million daily users. That work has also made me a named inventor on 15 patent-pending AI inventions, filed across the US and Canada.

On the side, I co-founded JustReva, a voice AI receptionist for healthcare clinics. With three teammates, we took it from an idea to a live product with paying customers, and I owned the product and the core build-vs-buy calls along the way. Building something from zero taught me more about product than any framework ever could.

I started my career as a data scientist at an early-stage startup, where I wore every hat. That experience gave me a deep technical foundation and a bias toward shipping over theorizing.

I think a lot about how AI changes distribution, why most AI products fail at the integration layer rather than the model layer, and what it means to build products where the AI is the experience rather than a feature bolted on.

Now

Leading AI platform strategy at TD Bank. Growing JustReva's pilot base across the US and Canada. Writing about voice AI and product-led growth. Looking for my next opportunity in AI product management.

Thinking

What I care about right now

LLM Distribution

How products get discovered through conversational AI interfaces. ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are becoming acquisition channels, and most companies aren't ready for it.

PLG + AI

Product-led growth meets AI-powered experiences. How do you build a funnel when the user's first interaction happens inside someone else's LLM?

Voice as Interface

Building JustReva taught me that voice AI is finally ready for production. The stack (ASR + LLM + TTS) is cheap enough and good enough to replace real workflows.

Experimentation Culture

The best product teams run experiments constantly. Not just A/B tests but real hypothesis-driven product discovery at every stage of the funnel.

Platform Partnerships

Building on third-party platforms (APIs, integrations, ecosystems) is a distribution superpower. The best PMs treat partnerships as a product surface.

0-to-1 Building

I'm drawn to the earliest stage of a product. When there's no playbook, no data, and the job is to figure out what to build and why anyone would care.
